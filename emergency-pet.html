When to seek emergency care for pets?

Very often we get asked what to look out for and when the right time is to bring a pet into a 24 hour vet? Here are some circumstances that would be grounds for seeking immediate medical attention:

  • Bite wounds
  • Changes in behavior
  • Toxin ingestion or poisoning
  • Your pet ate something that’s not edible (Ingestion of a foreign body)
  • Difficulty breathing
  • Bleeding
  • Hypothermia
  • Hyperthermia
  • Clear indications of pain (pacing, panting, limping, anxiousness)
  • Diarrhea
  • Difficulty urinating
  • Eye problems (redness, discharge, tear, etc.)
  • Ear infections
  • Swelling
  • Allergic reactions
  • Vomiting
  • Rash
  • Increase or decrease in appetite
  • Hair loss
  • Ingrown nails
  • Urinating blood
  • Seizures

FAQs

What to do if your animal is having an emergency?

If your animal has an emergency, don’t wait to call your vet. If you’re unsure what to do and can’t get in touch with a vet as quickly as possible, contact the nearest pet clinic or 24/7 emergency clinic.

If you can stabilize your pet and take them directly to the vet for treatment, this will help prevent further damage from occurring until help arrives.

What are the signs of emergency in dogs?

Emergency signs in dogs can include vomiting, diarrhea, and lethargy. Other symptoms include seizures and collapse. If your dog seems unable to stand or walk on its own, it may also have difficulty breathing or coughing up blood.

The best way to determine if your pet needs emergency care is by speaking with a veterinarian who has experience treating animals with similar conditions as yours.

What to do if your pet eats something toxic?

If your pet eats something poisonous, call the vet right away. Your pet could be in serious trouble if they have ingested a potentially fatal substance.

Your veterinarian will give you instructions on what to do next, including how long it will take to get results from laboratory testing and how long they should stay hospitalized while they recover from the poisoning.
